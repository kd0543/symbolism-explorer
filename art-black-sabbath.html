Black Sabbath

A storm-blue face breaks into a scream—like a church bell struck with lightning. The palette fights between cold blues and raw flesh reds; the mouth becomes a tunnel, a siren, a furnace. This one lives where dread turns sacramental: fear transfigured into awe.

This composition explores spiritual rupture, ecstatic dread, and symbolic transformation through violent chromatic contrast. The screaming form functions almost like a ritual mask—an embodiment of existential shock that converts terror into revelation.
